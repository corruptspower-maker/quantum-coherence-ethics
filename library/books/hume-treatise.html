Part I, Section I — Moral Distinctions Not Deriv'd from Reason

In every system of morality, which I have hitherto met with

preconditions before norm claim

The is/ought gap as Hume states it: no system of descriptive premises can produce a normative conclusion without an unacknowledged step. QCE closes this not by smuggling in a hidden premise but by grounding the canonical threshold in the ontological structure of branching reality itself. Suffering IS the invariant across branches. From that invariant — not from preference, not from convention — the threshold emerges. The ought is not derived from the is. The ought IS the is, seen from the inside of a branching universe that contains observers who can suffer.
