On the Infinite Universe and Worlds

Giordano Bruno

Dialogue 1

There are innumerable suns, and an infinite number of earths revolve around them, each with its own living beings and its own destiny.

infinite worlds as real branches

This passage directly parallels the Everettian many-worlds interpretation: all branches are equally real. QCE uses this as the ontological foundation for the distributed being, where each 'world' corresponds to a branch of the wave function.

Dialogue 2

The same soul, the same intellect, unfolds itself in infinite ways through infinite worlds, yet remains one in its essence.

distributed unity across worlds

Bruno describes a single entity instantiated across multiple worlds — the same distributed being QCE posits across branches. This resonates with Spectral Identity: every wavelength is the light, and identity is not dissolved but distributed.

Dialogue 3

Corruption and decay are not defects of nature but necessary features of its infinite expression, for every world contains its own order and its own dissolution.

suffering as structural fact

Bruno treats decay as an ontological feature, not a moral failing. QCE similarly treats suffering as a structural reality within branches — the entropic asymptote is not a punishment but a description of what happens without Triple P investment.

Dialogue 4

Reason does not prescribe what ought to be; it discovers what is, and in the infinite, is and ought coincide at the deepest level of nature.

is/ought convergence in infinity

Bruno hints at a naturalistic resolution of the is/ought gap: in an infinite structure, the conditions that make ethics possible are not imposed from outside but are given by the physics itself. QCE identifies Triple P as the floor where 'ought' regains meaning without crossing Hume's guillotine.

Dialogue 5

The infinite is the measure of all things, and no finite action can exhaust its potential; what is left undone in one world is completed in another.

non-finitude and canonical threshold

Bruno's claim that no finite action exhausts infinite potential echoes QCE's non-finitude: the distributed being is bounded but inexhaustible. The canonical equation describes the weight of present actions across branches — no single act forecloses the whole distribution, yet every act shapes inherited conditions.
