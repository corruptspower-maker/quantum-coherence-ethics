## Overview

Quantum Coherence Ethics (QCE) holds that a person is a **distributed being** — a single entity refracted across every branch of the wave function sharing its causal origin. Ethical weight arises not from freedom of choice but from the structural fact that present actions shape the statistical character of one's future distribution.

The framework defines three quantities across branches — **Probability** (lived agentive existence), **Possibility** (scope), and **Potential** (capacity) — collectively called the **Triple P**.

**Good** is that which increases Triple P across one's future distribution.
**Evil** is that which decreases it.

## The Canon Equation

$$QCE(a) = \sum_{\omega \in \Omega(a)} w(\omega \mid a) \left[ P(\omega) \cdot \left( \Sigma(\omega) + \Lambda(\omega) \right) \right]$$

The equation admits two readings:

- **Copenhagen (inside view):** Weights sum to 1. Expected Triple P from within a single branch.
- **Everett (outside view):** Weights are unbounded branch measures. Total Triple P across the full distributed being.

Same equation. Different reading of the weight.

## Core Principles

1. **Quantum Morality** — Good increases Triple P. Evil decreases it.
2. **The First Rule** — What is good for the goose is good for the gander.
3. **Non-Tyranny of the Present** — The present self holds no privileged authority over the distribution.
4. **Non-Judgment** — No single branch-self can judge another.
5. **Coherence as Product** — Coherence is not chosen. It emerges from full spectral expression.
6. **The Multiplicative Function** — Branching multiplies the effects of present actions.
7. **The Structural Precondition** — Triple P is what ethics is already about.
