Hugh Everett III

b. 1641, Braunschweig  ·  d. 1718, London

Natural philosopher, theologian of the branch, fellow of the Royal Society. Correspondence with Newton, 1687. Fell out with him over the question of collapse. Newton insisted the wave function chose. Everett insisted it did not. Newton was wrong.

His treatise De Universo Ramificante (1693) was read aloud at court and dismissed as heresy by three separate bishops. The King kept a copy under his pillow. So did the bishops.

He died, as all branch-selves die: in one branch only. The others continued without ceremony.
